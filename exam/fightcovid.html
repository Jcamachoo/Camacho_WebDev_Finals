About covid

What is Covid19?

Coronaviruses are a huge group of viruses that are common among animals. In uncommon cases, they are what researchers call zoonotic, meaning they can be transmitted from animals to a person, according to the US Centers for Illness Control and Avoidance.

How it started?

Specialists say SARS-CoV-2 begun in bats. SARS-CoV-2 made the jump to people at one of Wuhan’s open-air “wet markets.” They’re where customers purchase new meat and fish, including animals that are slaughtered on the spot. As SARS-CoV-2 spread both inside and outside China, it contaminated individuals who have had no direct contact with animals. That implied the virus is transmitted from one human to another. It’s presently spreading within the U.S. and around the globe, meaning that individuals are unwittingly catching and passing on the coronavirus. This developing around the world transmission is what is presently a pandemic.

HOW CAN IT SPREAD

Sharing of food or drinks

Since there's almost certain to be saliva involved in any sharing of drinks, salivary transfer of germs/viruses/etc. is going to happen.

Droplet Transmission

A virus-filled particle of breath or drool that comes out of the nose or mouth of a contaminated person when they breathe, talk, cough or sneeze. Droplets generally drop to the ground within a number of feet of the individual who expels them.

Person to Person

Person-to-person contact appears to be the most method of transmission of the novel coronavirus. Usually happens when: Somebody with the infection sneezes or coughs on you, leaving respiratory droplets on your skin or clothing, otherwise you touch somebody who has the infection on their skin or clothing.
